# EDITORIAL HISTORY

Somerton Man Intelligence Dossier

Date established: 2026-07-09

## Purpose

This document records the editorial history, governing principles, and revision policy for the Somerton Man Intelligence Dossier.

It is intended to function as the project's editorial constitution: a concise statement of how the dossier was developed, how revisions are controlled, and under what circumstances future editions should be issued.

## Version History

### v0.x

Research development and repository formation.

- Evidence collection, source acquisition, and register construction.
- Initial LaTeX manuscript architecture.
- Verification, chronology, and publication-governance foundations.

### v1.0.0

First publication edition.

- Established the investigative foundation.
- Published the first integrated dossier structure.
- Separated evidence, source mapping, chronology, and analytical layers.

### v1.0.1

Scholarly editorial revision.

- Strengthened methodology, historiography, and source criticism.
- Clarified evidentiary boundaries, uncertainty treatment, and analytical safeguards.
- Added change-register and quality-assurance documentation.

### v1.1.0

Editorial and Design Edition.

- Improved typography, layout, navigation, and publication presentation.
- Added a formal reader preface and design-governance artifacts.
- Preserved the frozen substantive content while elevating professional readability.

## Editorial Principles

- Evidence-first.
- Primary-source preference.
- Explicit uncertainty.
- Confidence proportional to evidence.
- No sensationalism.
- Traceable revisions.
- Version-controlled publication.
- Conflicts preserved rather than harmonized by assumption.
- Absence of evidence not treated as evidence of absence without source support.

## Editorial Freeze

Version 1.1.0 is the current editorially frozen edition.

No further substantive revision should be made unless one of the revision triggers below is met. Routine tinkering after this point risks diminishing returns, inconsistency, or regression in a project whose principal arguments and presentation have already been stabilized.

## Future Revision Policy

Revise only for:

- new primary evidence
- official findings
- demonstrable factual error
- authenticated new forensic evidence
- significant scholarly developments

## Major Revision Triggers

The following events would justify a major analytical revision, including a possible Version 2.0.0:

- publication of South Australian Coroner's findings
- release of additional SAPOL material
- authenticated new forensic evidence
- release of significant new primary-source archival material

## Independent Review Policy

Independent review is the preferred next step after Version 1.1.0.

Reviewers should be asked to identify:

- factual concerns
- unsupported reasoning
- missing context
- ambiguity
- structural weaknesses

Review should be sought from multiple disciplines where possible, including combinations of:

- forensic pathology
- history
- intelligence analysis
- archival practice
- general readability review by a subject-matter-informed but case-unfamiliar reader

## Repository Governance Note

The dossier should be accompanied by its supporting editorial and quality artifacts, including:

- change registers
- quality-assurance reports
- editorial assessments
- style and design guidance
- publication-readiness assessments

Together these documents form part of the research artifact. They do not guarantee that every conclusion is correct, but they make the dossier materially easier to evaluate, critique, and revise responsibly.
