# RESEARCH SCOPE

Somerton Man Intelligence Dossier

Date established: 2026-07-09

## Purpose

This repository documents an evidence-based historical, forensic, and intelligence-style assessment of the Somerton Man case.

Its purpose is to preserve a transparent research record, a publication-ready scholarly edition, and the supporting evidentiary and editorial infrastructure required for later review, critique, or revision.

## Non-Objectives

- Not intended to prove a preferred theory.
- Not intended to promote speculation.
- Not intended to replace primary records.
- Not intended as legal advice.
- Not intended as fictional reconstruction or popular true-crime narrative.

## Scope

Included:

- historical reconstruction
- forensic assessment
- intelligence analysis
- source criticism
- historiography
- evidentiary weighting
- uncertainty and confidence assessment
- versioned publication workflow

Excluded:

- unsupported speculation
- fictional reconstruction
- conspiracy narratives
- psychological profiling beyond available evidence
- conclusions not supported by the documented record
- rhetorical closure where the evidence remains unresolved

## Evidentiary Boundary

The repository gives greater weight to contemporary documentary evidence, official records, and source-mapped primary material than to later recollections, derivative summaries, unsupported public claims, or interpretive narratives.

Conflicting evidence is preserved where appropriate rather than harmonized by assumption.

Absence of evidence is not treated as evidence of absence unless a reliable source establishes that such an inference is warranted.

## Repository Status

The repository is content frozen as of Version 1.1.0.

Version 1.1.0 is the current edition of record unless and until a qualifying revision trigger is met.

## Revision Policy

Future revisions require one or more of the following:

- new primary evidence
- official findings
- demonstrable factual correction
- authenticated new forensic evidence
- major scholarly developments

## Stewardship Note

This repository should now be treated as a governed scholarly edition rather than an open-ended drafting project.

Future work should branch from the frozen edition of record rather than revise it casually in place. That approach preserves the integrity of the publication, the editorial trail, and the historical record it documents.
