\chapter*{About This Investigation}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{About This Investigation}

This dossier was created as an evidence-first review of the Somerton Man case. Its purpose is to separate documented facts, supported interpretations, working hypotheses, speculation, and unresolved intelligence gaps so that readers can see what the record supports and what remains unknown.

The project differs from a conventional narrative account by treating the manuscript as the final output of a structured research repository. The underlying work includes source registers, document inventories, claim verification records, chronology tables, witness and exhibit indexes, forensic-medical registers, physical-evidence catalogues, and structured analytic assessments.

This investigation does not advocate a preferred theory. It preserves uncertainty where the evidence is incomplete, conflicting, or unavailable, and it avoids resolving gaps by assumption. Confidence language is therefore intended to describe the quality and consistency of the evidence, not to imply certainty beyond the record.

The dossier is designed to be reproducible and revisable. Future editions may change if new official records, forensic results, archival material, or peer-reviewed analyses become available. Version 1.0.0 should be read as a documented assessment of the accessible evidence base at the time of publication, not as a final closure of the case.
