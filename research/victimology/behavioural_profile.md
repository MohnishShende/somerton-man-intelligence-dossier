# Behavioural Profile

Mission 7 rule: this file separates observed behaviours, witness observations, documented habits, inferred characteristics and unknowns. It does not diagnose, assign motive, or evaluate cause of death.

## Observed Behaviours

- A man was reported lying near the steps/seawall at Somerton Beach around 7:00 p.m. on 30 November 1948 and was seen to raise his right arm before it fell. Status: Verified as witness observation; identity not conclusively established. Source: DOC-0001 p.5; WIT-0001.
- Strapps and Neill reported seeing a man lying at the beach around 7:20-8:00 p.m., with no observed movement. Status: Verified as witness observation; identity not conclusively established. Sources: DOC-0001 pp.31-33,51; WIT-0006; WIT-0009.
- Body position on discovery was described by Moss and in coroner remarks. Status: Verified as post-discovery observation. Sources: DOC-0001 pp.5,19.

## Witness Observations About Appearance

- Clothing was described as fairly good quality in coroner remarks. Source: DOC-0001 p.5.
- Dwyer described the body as in good physical condition, with cared-for fingernails and feet. Source: DOC-0001 p.35.
- Leane and other testimony described clothing, shoes, physical build, hair and dental absence. Sources: DOC-0001 pp.57,67.
- John Burton Cleland described cleanliness/appearance and shoe condition. Source: DOC-0001 p.81.

## Documented Habits

- Smoking is suggested by cigarette evidence and finger staining in Leane testimony, but habit-level wording requires manual transcription before final use. Status: Partially Verified. Sources: DOC-0001 pp.19,57,69.
- Grooming/cleanliness is supported as witness/medical observation, not a personality conclusion. Sources: DOC-0001 pp.35,81.

## Inferred Characteristics

No inferred characteristic is promoted to fact in Mission 7. Possible occupation, education, social class, travel purpose, intent, planning, or technical skill remain unknown unless supported by future primary evidence.

## Unknowns

- Identity and verified life history.
- Residence, family, employer, friends and associates.
- Purpose of travel or presence at Somerton.
- Whether the man seen on the evening of 30 November was definitively the deceased.
- Who removed any clothing tags and when.
- Why the Tamam Shud slip was in the fob pocket.
- Whether suitcase contents prove ownership by the deceased beyond the inquest-level association.
