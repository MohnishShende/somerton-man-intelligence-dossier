# Style Guide

## Voice

Use restrained intelligence-assessment prose. Prefer clarity over drama.

Avoid:

- Sensational language
- Unnecessary certainty
- Theory-first framing
- Loaded verbs such as "proved" unless the evidence standard is met

Prefer:

- "The record indicates..."
- "Available evidence supports..."
- "This remains unresolved..."
- "A competing interpretation is..."

## Analytical Labels

Use explicit labels where needed:

- Established Fact
- Analytical Assessment
- Working Hypothesis
- Speculative Scenario

## Citations

Every factual paragraph should be traceable to citations or a verification table. Page numbers, image numbers, or archive locators are preferred.

## Tables

Tables should be compact, source-linked, and readable in print. Avoid decorative formatting.

## Figures

Figures should serve evidentiary or explanatory purposes. Each figure requires a caption, source, date, rights note, and processing note.

## Typography

The LaTeX design uses restrained color, generous margins, clear headings, and professional spacing. Avoid visual effects that imply certainty or drama.
